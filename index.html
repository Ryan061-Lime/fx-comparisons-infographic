Demystifying Business FX Costs

A visual guide comparing the FX pricing models of traditional banks and modern fintech providers for spot and forward trades.

The Real Cost of Spot Trades

An FX Spot Trade is an immediate currency exchange. While seemingly straightforward, the costs can vary dramatically. Traditional banks often apply wide, tiered spreads, especially for smaller volumes, while fintechs may offer tighter rates but with specific fees and conditions.

The chart opposite compares the estimated percentage cost for a business to exchange €10,000.

Hedging with Forward Contracts

Forward Contracts lock in an exchange rate for a future date, protecting your business from currency fluctuations. The cost is typically embedded in the forward rate or charged as an explicit fee. Transparency on these costs is key.

This chart visualizes the known fees or maximum margins for forward contracts.

Key Takeaways for Your Business

💡 Prioritize Total Cost

Look beyond "zero fee" marketing. The real cost lies in the final exchange rate. Always compare the final amount you receive or pay across providers.

🤝 Negotiate for Volume

For large or frequent trades, engage directly with traditional banks or discuss custom plans with fintechs like Revolut. Significant savings are possible through negotiation.

📱 Leverage Digital Platforms & Support

For businesses of all sizes, fintech platforms offer efficiency and competitive rates. Revolut's blend of self-service and expert consultancy provides flexible support for your FX needs. Watch out for weekend markups.

📈 Understand Forward Contracts

When hedging, remember the cost is baked into the forward rate itself, not just in explicit fees. Compare the actual forward rates offered for a true cost analysis.

🧩 Match Provider to Need

Revolut, with its transparent pricing, scalable custom plans, and expert support, is a strong contender across all business sizes. For highly specialized or complex financial instruments, traditional banks may still offer unique solutions.

❓ Demand Transparency

If a provider like Convera or Ulster Bank claims "competitive pricing" without showing numbers, press for a detailed quote showing all spreads and fees before you trade.
